Why do we default to consuming when creating feeds the soul? Is there a
biological reason (energy preservation?), or is it a product of social
structures and the constant competition for our attention?

I suppose I'm a bit of an odd duck in that I often prefer creation over
consumption. In fact, a lot of the more passive activities that normies do for
entertainment I find to be boring and unfulfilling. I like to make things,
create, explore. I don't generally enjoy being a tourist, watching sports,
excessive hedonism, and so forth. In fact, many of the things normal people
consider to be "fun" are my idea of a bad time (almost like punishment).

This definitely makes it challenging for me to relate to others, but I'm
certainly not alone and I've met plenty of creatives like myself.
